## Framework: The 6 Layers of Content

Every presentation — whether 60-min preview, 90-min webinar, or 3-day workshop — must have all 6 layers:

### Layer 1: FRAMING
> "Framing puts your audience in the right frame of mind. If you've done that well, the rest of your presentation is going to be smooth sailing."

- Pull all individuals into a **singular frame of mind** before content begins
- Say something without saying something — use framing to pre-handle resistance
- Use facts, news, stories, statistics to reinforce the frame you want
- **Rules and culture** are framing — set expectations at the very beginning
- Frame your price (compare to MBA, compare to Starbucks coffee)
- Frame risk (bungee jumping is 25x safer than driving)
- Framing must happen **before** content, not during

**Key test:** Before you start, do your audience think what you want them to think? Have they agreed to what you want them to agree?

### Layer 2: STORY
> "Logic means 0 and 1. Buying behavior is 90-95% emotional, not logical."

**Why story works:** Emotional connection → influence → purchase. You cannot influence through logic alone.

**4 Types of Stories:**
1. **Own life story** — most powerful; personal, authentic
2. **Other people's stories** — "I heard from my friend..."
3. **Current/major impact stories** — news, trending events
4. **Fictional stories** — clearly labeled, but conveys powerful point (e.g. the monkey experiment)

**Vince's Story Structure (demonstrated in "Send Us to USA" story):**
- Establish the before-state (struggling, didn't believe)
- The obstacle/setback (monkeys laughing, embarrassment)
- The decision to persist despite mockery
- The result (US$69,000 in 7 days)
- The moral (stop listening to monkeys)
- **Indirect selling**: story pre-handles the objection "my family/friends will say don't do it"

**Key storytelling techniques:**
- Add enough detail so the audience is **imagining** the scene
- Stories should convey key points without stating them directly
- Multiple stories for same moral = Rule of 3 applied to narrative
- Story blocks: collect and categorize stories by the objection/point they address

### Layer 3: OBJECTION
> "Every no is an opportunity to get closer to a yes. Objection means progress."

**Sources of objections to research:**
- Call/interview customers who DIDN'T buy (gold — painful but invaluable)
- Survey prospects
- Talk to existing customers about why they bought
- Research online forums, complaints, reviews in your niche
- Common misconceptions and false beliefs in your industry

**Types of objections:**
- Common misconceptions (things they believe that aren't true)
- False beliefs (internal stories they tell themselves)
- Universal objections: money, time, confidence, alternatives, procrastination

**Key insight:** Longer touchpoint = more time to eliminate objections = higher-priced offers become possible. A 12-hour workshop can sell what a 90-min preview cannot.

### Layer 4: VALUE & INSIGHT
- The actual teaching/content
- Must connect to the pain and objections already established
- Teach in a way that makes them want MORE (open loops)
- Every insight should be tied back to the offer

### Layer 5: OFFER
See Offer Framework below.

### Layer 6: CLOSE
See Close/Commitment Framework below.

## Business Model Frameworks

### The Ascension Model (with DIY/DWY/DFY Layer)

```
FREE CONTENT (DIY) → LOW TICKET (DIY) → MID TICKET (DWY) → HIGH TICKET (DFY/DWY)
```

- **DIY** = Do It Yourself (books, videos, free content, recorded workshops)
- **DWY** = Do With You (group coaching, live workshops, community)
- **DFY** = Done For You (high-end service, personal coaching, done-for-you delivery)

**Pricing strategy:** Always start aggressive/competitive → over-deliver → optimize price upward over time. Never jump straight to high price with zero data.

### Funnel Models

**Preview Model:**
```
Traffic → Opt-in → 90-min Preview → Sell Ticket → Workshop → Backend High Ticket
```
- Advantages: Paid attendees = higher quality; preview can cover ad costs before workshop
- Disadvantages: Cuts down attendance pool (only 20% of preview attendees buy ticket)

**Free Workshop Model:**
```
Traffic → Opt-in → Free Workshop → Backend High Ticket (via consultative close)
```
- Advantages: 5x more people in workshop; more interview registrations; more high-ticket sales
- Disadvantages: High pressure on workshop delivery; no revenue before workshop

**Math comparison (per 1,000 leads):**
- Preview model: 300 attend preview → 60 buy ticket → 6-8 high-ticket sales
- Free workshop: 300 attend workshop → 100 interviews → 15-20 high-ticket sales

**Triple-Offer Workshop (advanced):**
- Day 1: Sell low ticket ($X) → leads to secondary workshop
- Day 2: Sell mid ticket ($XX) → leads to another workshop
- Last day: Sell high ticket ($XXXXX)
- Each low/mid ticket workshop also pitches back to high ticket = 3 bites at high-ticket

## Offer Design Framework

### The Offer Stack (Pressure Cooker Model)
> "Creating a pressure cooker. As you stack the offer, you're building up pressure. Throw in guarantee — no brainer, no risk. Drop the price — pressure goes off the roof."

**Stack sequence:**
1. Main offer (with WOW factor, specific pain it solves)
2. Bonuses (each presented as powerfully as main offer)
3. Fast action bonus (creates FOMO momentum)
4. Guarantee (removes risk)
5. Price reveal (after full value is established)

### Main Offer Presentation
- Visual representation (digital box/mockup) — critical for online
- Overview, benefits, WOW factor
- Who is it for / prerequisites
- Testimonials specifically for the offer
- **Mention 1-2 specific wow elements** that address their biggest pain point
- Generic > specific: "learn how to build business" = weak; "the one weird 8-figure method using other people's money" = strong

### Bonus Framework
- Purpose: create perceived value, avoid confusing main offer, speed up results, remove false beliefs, crush objections
- **Present bonuses with the same effort as the main offer** — never treat as afterthought
- Bonuses can have their own testimonials
- Best bonus = one that makes people buy the whole offer JUST for the bonus
- Types: templates, community, coaching calls, software, compilations, swipes, case studies, SOPs, partnerships
- **Achievement bonus**: reward them for getting results (motivates implementation)
- Fast action bonus: creates momentum, group momentum, first N people only — MUST honour it

## Performance Tracking (Vince's KPI System)

### Preview Tracking
- Ad spend, leads, CPL
- Sales during preview vs. after preview
- Attendance: start / at pitch / at CTA
- Peak attendees
- Retention rate (must be 90-95% minimum)
- Sales conversion, total revenue, ROAS

### Workshop Tracking
- Ad spend, leads, CPL
- Attendance by day (count those who attended 30+ min)
- Interview registration by day
- Sales by day
- Interviews completed vs. dropout
- Full payment vs. part payment (if >70% full pay → price too low, increase price)
- Total collection vs. total sales (uncollected = warning sign)
- 5-star breakdown on feedback forms
- **Compare workshops side by side in columns**

### Key Insight on Data
> "If 100 people pay full payment, that tells you you priced it too low — increase your price."

> "Understanding customer pain is important and profitable. The more you understand your customer, the better the ad and the lower the cost."

**Process for finding winning ad angle:**
1. Call 100 customers who attended — discover 5-10 reasons they came
2. Create one ad for each pain angle
3. Spend $500 on each ad
4. Find the one with lowest CPL → triple down everything on that ad

| **Objection Addressed** | Did the story pre-handle a specific buying objection? |

### Objection Handling (Layer 3)
| Area | What to Look For |
|------|-----------------|
| **Objections Addressed** | Were common objections addressed during content? |
| **Misconceptions Killed** | Were false beliefs challenged and corrected? |

### Offer & Close
| Area | What to Look For |
|------|-----------------|
| **Pressure Stack** | Did value stack in sequence before price reveal? |
| **WOW Element Mentioned** | At least 1 specific, compelling offer element highlighted? |
| **Bonus Quality** | Bonuses presented with same energy as main offer? |
| **Fast Action Bonus** | Momentum-creating bonus present? |
| **Guarantee** | Risk removed before price revealed? |
| **Data Tracking** | Any mention of numbers/results from past students? |

### Red Flags (Vince Tan)
- No framing at opening — audience not aligned before content starts
- Story absent or generic — no emotional connection established
- Offer presented as a list, not a pressure cooker
- Bonuses treated as afterthoughts — mentioned without drama
- No guarantee — maximum friction at close
- No fast action bonus — no reason to decide now
- Generic proof ("many people succeeded") vs. specific case studies with numbers
- Price revealed before value fully stacked
- Energy drops during offer section — must maintain or increase energy at close

## Mind Control MasterClass Framework (Days 1-2)

> "Make them believe and agree that during that one and a half hour, you are the only choice."

### The 3 Pre-Presentation Mindsets
Before stepping in front of any audience, Vince demands three mental states be locked in:

1. **Certainty** — "The person with the highest certainty in a conversation will win the deal." Your doubt resonates at a frequency the audience can feel. Certainty must come from genuinely believing your product is the best in its segment/niche/market.
2. **Serving** — Go in to help, not to extract money. This changes your words, tonality, and energy. Audiences feel the difference.
3. **Leading** — You own the stage. Most people want to be led. If you don't lead, the audience will, and you lose control of the sale.

### Setting the Stage (Before Content Starts)
Three things must happen before you present substance:

1. **Set the Rules** — State 3 rules with explanations: Participate (why: you remember better), Go All In (why: no one succeeds at 50%), Take Action (why: only results matter). This frames the audience to comply before content starts.
2. **WIIFY (What's In It For You)** — Give the audience something to look forward to. Preview key takeaways. Reward them for staying to the end.
3. **WIIFM (What's In It For Me) + Transparency** — Be honest about your agenda. Never hide that you'll pitch. "Hiding your agenda is one of the dumbest things. It will reduce your conversion." Honesty about intent = trust = higher conversion.

### Framing — The Master Tool
> "One of the biggest failures of why people are not able to convince someone is because a lot of people think that the buying decision happens in the last 10 minutes. No — it happens from the first second."

**What is Framing:**
Framing puts your audience into the mindset you need for the rest of your presentation to work. Done well, it makes the close easy. Done poorly, the audience is "all over the place" with 50 different questions.

**6 Types of Framing:**
| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Situation** | "More millionaires created during COVID than ever before" — frames business launch as opportunity |
| **Attribute** | Reframe fat/healthy, risky/safe, slow/thorough — depends on what you're selling |
| **Action** | Robin Hood: good or bad? Depends on your frame |
| **Risk** | "Bungee jumping risk of death = 1 in 500,000; Car = 1 in 20,000. So bungee is 25x safer than driving" |
| **Limit** | Speed limits are bad on Autobahn, good on regular roads |
| **Responsibility** | Reframe family obligation as motivation to invest |

**4 Framing Tools:**
1. **Metaphor** — "Entrepreneur Masterclass costs less than a Starbucks coffee per day" — compares price to relatable anchor
2. **Story** — The monkey experiment, the Send Us to USA story — both frame indirectly, carry hidden messages
3. **Tradition** — "Go to school, get a job, get a car, get a house, die" — frames the rat race as a problem, entrepreneurship as the exit
4. **Slogan/Jargon/Catchphrase** — "Yes we can" (Obama), "Make America great again" (Trump) — instant associative anchoring
5. **Contrast** — "We normally charge $10,000/day but today just $1,000" — makes $1,000 feel cheap
6. **Spin** — "67% chance of success" = "33% chance of failure" — same fact, different frame

**Framing Process:**
1. Identify the main goal (what mindset/feeling/agreement do you want?)
2. Choose the right tool (metaphor, story, tradition, contrast, spin?)
3. Craft the framing content

**Key Test:** Before you start content — do they think what you want them to think? Have they already agreed to what you want?

### The SPLASH Story Framework
Every high-impact story has 6 elements:

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **S — Situation** | Show the world BEFORE the problem — so the problem makes sense |
| **P — Problem** | Define the problem — but hero can't solve it yet |
| **L — Light Bulb** | Key insight arrives — doesn't solve yet, but awakens awareness |
| **A — Awareness** | Hero revisits past + new insight = understands WHERE they went wrong |
| **S — Solution** | How the problem was finally solved |
| **H — Highlight** | What was learned? What transformation occurred? |

**4 Story Types (ranked best to last):**
1. Own life story (most powerful — personal, authentic)
2. Other people's stories
3. Current/major impact stories (news, events)
4. Fictional/made-up stories (label them clearly)

**5 Story Enhancement Techniques:**
1. **Open Loop** — Start a story without closing it; audience stays in suspense. Rule: First In, Last Out (close loops in reverse order of opening). Max 5-7 open loops in 90 min.
2. **Seeding** — Drop hints of what you'll pitch without directly saying it. "CK came to my workshop three times" seeds that Vince has a workshop without announcing it.
3. **Self-Conclusion** — Tell A + B; they conclude C themselves. "90% of Fortune 500 are entrepreneurs + you want to be rich = you should be an entrepreneur." Their inner voice tells them, not you.
4. **Total Flip Over** — Build storyline toward one conclusion, then flip to opposite. Deeply memorable. (The "ghost is actually human" movie twist effect.)
5. **Derail (Weakness as Strength)** — Reveal weakness openly and reframe it as honesty, freshness, or advantage. "I've never done property developer digital marketing — so I have no tunnel vision."

### The 3 Core False Beliefs (Objection Framework)
*Credited to Russell Brunson; Vince's adapted version:*

1. **Vehicle** — Is this the right product/method to get them where they want to go? (Must establish entrepreneurship/your solution IS the vehicle.)
2. **Internal** — Do they believe in their own ability to use it? ("I have no money to start a business" → case study of RM0 to RM20M business)
3. **External** — Are there external factors they can't control that will stop them? (Economy, family, time → must pre-handle with stories)
4. **Secret #4 (Vince addition)** — A combo secret that resolves all three false beliefs at once. Hard to create but highly powerful.

*For each secret: State the common false belief → Reveal the truth/new belief → Case study proof → Repeat the new belief.*

### Offer Stack Deep Dive (Mind Control Version)
> "The offer must be so damn good that if your prospect don't buy, they will feel so damn stupid."

**Bonus Design Principles:**
- Bonuses that **enhance** the main offer beat bonuses that are random
- Bonuses can **handle secondary false beliefs** (internal/external) that weren't addressed in secrets 1-3
- Use **conditional bonuses** ("When you lose 5 kilos, we'll give you Level 2 free") — this assumes success, creates pull, generates testimonials
- The power of **"when" vs "if"**: "When you lose 5 kilos" = assume they will. "If" = creates doubt.
- Partner bonuses: Tie up with businesses with high margins (aesthetic clinics, services) — they can give high-perceived-value bonuses cheaply
- **Physical bonuses** (especially unique ones from China) can be very powerful and cheap (reusable notebook, never-ending pencil, silver-thread socks)
- The best bonus = one where people would buy the whole offer JUST for that bonus

**Fast Action Bonus Rules:**
- Purpose: create momentum; prevent 50% of sales from disappearing after audience leaves
- "50% of sales are lost when action isn't taken in the moment; another 50% of the remaining 50% are lost within 24 hours"
- Must honor it — if you say 10 people, make it 10 people. Adjust price slightly if reopening, don't just ignore the close.
- Creates FOMO legitimately — not fake urgency

### Closing Methods (Vince's 5-Stage System)

**1. Progressive Close** — Break the path from "interested" to "paid" into micro-steps.
- Online: direct to page → "can you see it loading?" → "enter this discount code" → "can you see the price change?" → "proceed to register"
- Stage: verbal yes → raise hand → stand up → follow me to back → handed the form
- Converts 5-7x more than just saying "go sign up"

**2. Filter Close** — Filter out disinterested before pitching.
- Announce: "I have something to offer, but only to those interested in [specific outcome]. If not, enjoy your break."
- Interested go to breakout room/separate area
- No bad taste left; they volunteered to hear the pitch

**3. Form Close** — Use a physical object to anchor the purchase.
- Hand the form personally (Vince would hand 200 forms personally, nobody else allowed)
- Online version: scroll through sales page live, "happening to" point out testimonials and proof as they scroll to the buy button

**4. Interview Close** — For mid-to-high ticket items.
- Qualifies and filters: who is right/wrong fit
- Allows pivoting (wrong product → right product for them)
- Each interview = data for improving your presentation
- "30 interviews will give you more insights than any market research"
- Rule: Never incentivize interview attendance with gifts

**5. Stage Close** — Present to one person on stage, then open to crowd.
- "Only one person gets this offer. Marcus, you want it or not?" Others watching get social proof. If Marcus says yes, "who else wants it?" — everyone rushes.
- Very powerful but requires practice and correct person selection
- Vince used this successfully multiple times even when 3 speakers used same method in same event
