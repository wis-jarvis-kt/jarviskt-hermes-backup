## Core Principle

> "Because emotion decides action, not logic."

**The technique:** Same factual meaning, opposite emotional priming.  
- **Negative annotation** → shuts down action (fear, shame, avoidance, regret)  
- **Positive annotation** → opens possibility and commitment (hope, empowerment, strategic identity)

The speaker's job is NOT to change the facts — it's to **frame the same reality in a way that moves people toward action**.

---

## The Two Annotations

| Annotation | Emotional State Triggered | Result |
|------------|--------------------------|--------|
| **Negative** | Fear, shame, guilt, avoidance, inferiority | Paralysis, resistance, defensiveness |
| **Positive** | Hope, empowerment, opportunity, identity upgrade | Openness, commitment, buying intent |

## Core Examples (Workshop-Applicable)

### Financial Situation
- ❌ "Your finances are a mess — if you don't change, it'll get worse."
- ✅ "You've reached the perfect stage for a reset. One small adjustment and your finances become more stable immediately."

### Progress / Readiness
- ❌ "You're still behind — people your age already started planning."
- ✅ "You're actually ahead of those who only wake up after a crisis. Starting now gives you a massive advantage."

### Discipline / Effort
- ❌ "You lack discipline — that's why your results are slow."
- ✅ "You've already built momentum. Imagine how fast results compound with just 10% more consistency."

### Past Mistakes
- ❌ "You made the wrong investment decisions."
- ✅ "You've gained the experience to know exactly what not to repeat. Your next move will be strategic."

### Identity Upgrade
- ❌ "You're stuck in employee mindset — that's why you can't scale."
- ✅ "You already have solid foundations as an employee. Add strategic thinking on top and you'll rise faster than most bosses."

### Not Ready Yet
- ❌ "You're slow at learning. Other people already automate 50% of their work."
- ✅ "You just haven't had the right structure yet. Once you have a system, you'll catch up faster than you think."

## Why This Matters for Workshop Speakers

A speaker using **negative annotation** unintentionally:
- Creates fear that paralyses rather than motivates
- Triggers shame that causes audience to disengage or defend
- Makes the audience feel "I can't do this" instead of "I want to do this"

A speaker using **positive annotation deliberately**:
- Maintains emotional safety while acknowledging reality
- Builds identity ("I am a strategic investor / I am a smart decision-maker")
- Moves audience from "I shouldn't" to "I should" to "I will"

## Scoring Criteria (Linguistic Priming Lens)

| Area | What to Look For |
|------|-----------------|
| **Positive vs Negative Ratio** | Does the speaker predominantly use positive annotation? |
| **Pain Delivery** | When pain is stated, is it framed as changeable (hope) or permanent (shame)? |
| **Identity Priming** | Are audience members primed to see themselves as capable/strategic? |
| **Reframe Quality** | When obstacles mentioned, are they reframed as advantages? |
| **Emotional State at Close** | Is the audience in an empowered state when offer is made? |
| **Language Precision** | Are specific trigger words used (rare, strategic, smart, advantage, momentum)? |
| **Audience Response** | Visible engagement/nods when positive annotation used? Silence/withdrawal on negative? |

### Red Flags
- Heavy use of shame language ("you should have done this already", "you're still behind")
- Pain without hope — describes problems but never pivots to empowerment
- Condescending framing ("most people fail because they're lazy")
- Identity attack without upgrade ("you have employee mindset" — stop there)
- Fear-based urgency without positive outcome framing
- Closing on a low-energy state — audience feeling inadequate right before offer
- Inconsistent annotation — positive during content, negative during close (kills momentum)
