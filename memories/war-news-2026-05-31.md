## Ukraine-Russia Conflict

### Russia losses nearly triple year-on-year; territorial advance stalls

**Source:** Al Jazeera — May 29, 2026  
**Category:** Ukraine-Russia War

**Summary:**  
Russia's rate of casualties in Ukraine has nearly tripled compared to last year, while its territorial gains have slowed to a near standstill in 2026. Ukrainian forces retook approximately 400 sq km in the Dnipropetrovsk region — the largest territorial gain since late 2022 — during Q1 2026.

**Key points:**
- Russia advanced a net 104 sq km between January 1 and May 26, 2026, vs. 1,619 sq km over the same period in 2025 (per ISW)
- Russian forces contested another 628 sq km but did not take control
- Ukraine says 145,000 Russian casualties this year: 86,000 killed, 59,000 seriously wounded
- Ukrainian Defence Minister: Russia lost 179 troops per sq km gained vs. 67 last year
- US DIA confirmed Russia has lost territory it previously occupied

**Why it matters:** The data suggests Russia's attritional strategy is becoming unsustainable — high losses with diminishing territorial returns. The slowdown in Russian advances may signal an inflection point as Ukraine receives additional Western support.

## Middle East: Israel-Gaza Conflict

### Netanyahu directs IDF to increase control of Gaza to 70%

**Source:** Reuters / BBC / CNN — May 28, 2026  
**Category:** Israel-Gaza War

**Summary:**  
Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu directed the IDF to increase its control of Gaza to 70% of the territory, raising alarm internationally. Hamas warned against escalation. Germany expressed alarm at Israel's plans for expanded territorial control.

**Key points:**
- Netanyahu ordered IDF to take and hold approximately 70% of Gaza
- Hamas called the directive "a dangerous escalation" and warned of consequences
- Germany summoned Israeli ambassador over the plans
- IDF operations continue with reported permissive open-fire rules along Gaza's Yellow Line
- Humanitarian conditions remain severe; UNRWA reports critical shortages

**Why it matters:** The 70% figure represents a significant shift toward indefinite Israeli control rather than post-war reconstruction planning. International allies including Germany are pushing back, increasing diplomatic isolation of Israel.

## South China Sea / Taiwan Strait

### China-Taiwan coast guard clash in disputed waters

**Source:** Stars and Stripes — May 26, 2026  
**Category:** South China Sea / Taiwan Strait

**Summary:**  
Chinese and Taiwanese coast guard ships clashed in disputed South China Sea waters, marking a significant escalation in maritime tensions. Separately, a Chinese research vessel departed after a tense standoff near Taiwan-controlled Itu Aba islands in the Spratly Islands.

**Key points:**
- China coast guard and Taiwan coast guard ships collided in disputed waters
- China has been shifting naval pressure beyond the Taiwan Strait into the broader South China Sea
- US allies (Japan, Australia, UK) are boosting Taiwan Strait transits; US has taken a different route
- Vietnam-China tensions also rising; ship-tracking data shows growing risk of confrontation
- Pentagon/SHADE forum: Hegseth tempered China criticism at Asia defence forum

**Why it matters:** China's coastal strategy is expanding from the Taiwan Strait to the full South China Sea, creating simultaneous pressure points across multiple disputing nations. The US posture shift (allies in, US less visible) signals strategic ambiguity may be deepening.
